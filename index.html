His dissertation was focused on developing of methodology of meta-omics (metagenomics, metatranscriptomics, metabolomics, etc.) to interpret the material exchange and bacterial interaction occurring among community member during microbial mediated biodegradation process. After his Ph.D. defense, he joined the University of California at Berkeley as a postdoctoral fellow in the Department of Civil and Environmental Engineering where he investigated material exchange and microbial interaction in important anaerobic bacteria (e.g TCE-decholorinating bacteria and anaerobic ammonium oxidation (anammox) bacteria) by using multi-omics and genomics techniques. He joined in as an Assistant Professor at the Environment and Energy Department at Peking University Shenzhen Graduate School in July 2016.
